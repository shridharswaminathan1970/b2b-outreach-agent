# B2B Outreach Agent — User Guide

A practical, end-to-end walkthrough of running an outreach initiative on the
platform, illustrated with a real worked example: **Orangekloud Technology
selling eMOBIQ** (its AI-orchestrated, NLP-driven app-development platform) to
mid-market manufacturing IT leaders.

Every AI email shown below was generated by the live system.

---

## 1. What the platform does

It runs a complete demand-generation outreach motion:

1. **Get contacts** — import a CSV or prospect with Apollo.
2. **Define the campaign** with a structured **Campaign Brief** (product + the
   four U's + buyer persona + sequence strategy).
3. The system **auto-builds a multi-touch sequence** — 80% trust-building
   touches, then 20% call-to-action touches at the end.
4. **AI drafts every email** personalized to the contact and grounded in your
   brief. Early touches deliver pure operational value (no sales pitch); the
   vendor appears only in the signature. The "ask" comes later.
5. **You review and approve** every draft — nothing sends automatically.
6. **Replies are classified** (interested, not interested, objection, referral,
   unsubscribe, auto-reply); interested replies can become **pipeline
   opportunities**; unsubscribes are suppressed instantly.
7. **Analytics and reports** track reply rate, meetings, and pipeline.

## 3. The worked example — Orangekloud selling eMOBIQ

> **The initiative:** Orangekloud's SDR team wants to book demos for **eMOBIQ**
> with IT leaders at 50–500-person manufacturers who have app backlogs and
> can't hire developers fast enough. They run a **9-touch campaign**: 7 trust
> touches that deliver genuinely useful operational intelligence, then a webinar
> invite and a demo offer.

### Step 1 — Get access (new company)

1. The prospect visits **`/signup`** and fills the request form (name, company,
   email, contact number, address, country).
2. The **Platform Owner** receives an email (`b2bo Agent super admin account
   creation`) and approves the request in the **Platform** console (`/platform`).
3. Approval provisions the company + a Super Admin user + demo data, and emails a
   **set-password link**.
4. The new Super Admin clicks the link, sets a password, and is **logged in
   automatically**.

> Already onboarded? Just sign in at **`/login`**.

### Step 2 — Tell the AI what you sell (Company settings)

Go to **Company** (`/settings`, Super Admin only) → **Settings (product / market
/ ICP — JSON)** and paste your product context. This drives every email's
framing and signature. For eMOBIQ:

```json
{
  "product": {
    "name": "eMOBIQ",
    "vendor": "Orangekloud Technology",
    "category": "AI-orchestrated low-code app development platform",
    "valueProp": "build deployable enterprise apps from plain-language descriptions, orchestrated by AI agents — no dev team required",
    "market": "mid-market manufacturing, logistics & field-service IT teams",
    "icp": "IT and digital-transformation leaders at 50–500 person firms with app backlogs and scarce developers"
  }
}
```

### Step 3 — Build your team (optional)

Under **Teams** and **Users**, a Super Admin or Team Manager creates teams and
adds Account Executives. Each user only sees the contacts/campaigns in their
scope. (Skip this for a solo run.)

### Step 4 — Get contacts

Two ways:

- **CSV import** — **Contacts** → import → preview → map fields → confirm.
  Duplicates are detected by email/LinkedIn.
- **Prospecting (Apollo)** — **Prospecting** → search by title, industry, company
  size, geography → import the results to Contacts.

For the eMOBIQ run: search Apollo for *"IT Director / Head of IT, Manufacturing,
50–500 employees, Singapore + SEA"* and import the matches.

### Step 5 — Create the Campaign Brief

**Campaigns** → **New campaign** (`/campaigns/new`). A 4-step builder:

**(a) Product Brief** — for eMOBIQ:

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Campaign name | eMOBIQ — Manufacturing IT Q3 |
| Product name | eMOBIQ |
| What does it do? | Describe an app in plain language; AI agents orchestrate the build into a deployable, enterprise-grade mobile/business app. |
| Who is it for? | IT and digital-transformation leaders at mid-market manufacturers |
| **Unworkable** | Custom business apps take months and scarce developers; backlogs pile up while business units wait. |
| **Urgent** | Digital-transformation pressure plus a developer shortage means competitors ship faster. |
| **Unavoidable** | Every quarter of delay means manual processes, shadow IT, and lost efficiency you can't hire your way out of. |
| **Underserved** | Traditional low-code still needs config experts; offshore dev is slow and risky; generic AI code tools don't produce deployable enterprise apps. |
| **Positioning** | The fastest path from idea to a production enterprise app — without a development team. |

**(b) Buyer Persona:** Industry *Manufacturing*, size *50–500*, economic buyer
*Director of IT* (seniority *Director*), optional co-decision maker *Operations
Director*.

**(c) Sequence Strategy:** set **Total touchpoints = 9**. The builder shows the
auto-split: **7 trust** (rotating Trust Builder → Value Add → Intel Gathering) +
**2 CTA** at the end. Configure the two CTA touches:

- **Touch 8 — Webinar:** *"From Idea to Enterprise App in Days: A Live eMOBIQ
  Build"*, date, registration link.
- **Touch 9 — Demo:** 30-min product demo, your booking link.

**(d) Review** → **Create campaign**. This creates the campaign (as a draft) plus
its sequence. You can review the brief and the touch plan anytime on the campaign
page.

### Step 6 — Enroll contacts

Open the campaign → the **sequence** → **enroll** your imported contacts (search,
select, choose "start now" or schedule). Suppressed contacts are skipped
automatically. Enrolling activates the campaign.

### Step 7 — AI generates drafts → you approve

The worker generates a personalized draft per contact per touch and puts each in
the **Drafts** review queue (`/drafts`). Open a card to read the subject + body,
a quality score, and the research brief. **Approve**, **edit**, or **reject**.
Nothing sends until you approve.

### Step 8 — Sending & suppression

Approved drafts send through your email provider. Before **every** send the
system checks the suppression list. A hard bounce or an unsubscribe suppresses
the contact immediately — they won't be contacted again.

### Step 9 — Replies, classification & pipeline

Incoming replies are auto-classified into six categories with a confidence
score. Low-confidence ones are flagged for human review in the **Replies** inbox
(`/replies`). From there:

- **Interested** → convert to an **Opportunity** (Pipeline, `/opportunities`)
  with a stage and amount; the deal workspace tracks it to close.
- **Unsubscribe** → suppressed instantly.
- **Objection / referral / not interested** → routed for the right follow-up.

When Marcus replies *"Sounds interesting, can you send times?"*, the system
classifies it **interested**, and you (or the worker) create an opportunity —
that demo is now in your forecast.

### Step 10 — Measure

- **Dashboard** (`/`) — reply rate, meetings booked, pipeline at a glance.
- **Analytics** (`/analytics`) — per-campaign reply rates and trends.
- **Pipeline** (`/opportunities`) — stages, amounts, forecast.
- **Audit log** (`/audit`) — every action on every entity, exportable as CSV.

## 4. Guardrails (built in)

- **Nothing auto-sends.** Every email is human-approved.
- **Trust before ask.** Early touches carry zero sales intent; the vendor is
  named only in the signature until the CTA touches.
- **Suppression is instant** on unsubscribe or hard bounce, checked before every
  send.
- **Full audit trail.** Every state change is logged and exportable.
